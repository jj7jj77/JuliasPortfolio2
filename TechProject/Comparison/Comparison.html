Comparison of Augmented and Virtual Reality
Augmented Reality Virtual Reality
Display Devices
Headsets optional-can include holodisplays and other tech Special headsets or smart glasses
Image Source
Combination of computer generated items and real life objects Computer graphics or real images produced by a computer
Environment
Both virtual and real life objects are seamlessly blended Fully digital
Perspective
Virtual objects behave based on user’s perspective in the real world Virtual objects will change position and size according to user’s perspective in the virtual world
Presence
Feeling of still being in the real world but with the new elements and objects super imposed Feeling of being transported somewhere else with no sense of the real world
Awareness
Virtual objects can be identified based on their nature and behavior such as floating text that follows a user Perfectly rendered virtual object can’t be
distinguished from the real deal
AR enhances what you see by adding computer-generated images to the view using different devices. VR provides users with a fully digital environment with no elements from reality.
Allows the user to see the world around them Does not allow the user to see the world around them
Requires the use of applications for smartphones and tablets Typically requires the use of wearing a specialized VR headset equipment.
Also some applications needing additional equipment as well, such as a handheld controller to gain the full benefit of the experience.
Requires upwards of 100 Mbps bandwidth A 720p VR video stream requires at least a 50 Mbps connection.
AR overlays graphics and animation on top of real-world environments Brings the users into a simulated reality by stimulating both sight of hearing (sound)
Needs lower costs to expand applications, therefore making more headway into the mainstream media. Adoption to technology is still limited, but inventors are looking for ways to make VR content more suitable for practical application
Users can add or change their perception of the world in real-time while easily distinguishing virtual items from existing ones. Users might experience issues with mobility when remaining fully immersed in the virtual environment, such as the inability to feel objects when 'picking' them up.
Standalone headsets / glasses Tethered VR headsets and standalone headsets
Head-mounted display that projects images and allows the user to see through its display. OHMDs are used in augmented reality (AR). Unlike Virtual Reality HMDs that obscures our vision of the real world, OHMDs allow us to see our surroundings while streaming data and image overlays in front of our eyes.

Categories of Immersive Reality

Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality are immersive technologies that integrates virtual and real world elements. They are more like technology cousins then twins which makes them sound similar, but they have their differences. As the technology has been developed over decades, they bleed over into each other a bit. They are two very different concepts though, with characteristics that can be readily distinguished from one another.

Augmented Reality

Augmented Reality stands for AR, is the blending of virtual reality and real life.
It is open and partly immersive.

How long has AR been around?

2016 had set the tone for the rise of Augmented Reality with the number of new smart glasses entering the market.

What is Mixed Reality

Mixed reality is the result of blending the physical world with the digital world. It is the next evolution in human, computer, and environment interaction and unleashes possibilities that until now were only restricted to our imaginations.

The device to use for mixed reality is HoloLens. It is a mixed reality HMD(Head-Mounted Display) made by Microsoft. This is a holographic computer that allows for increased productivity, collaboration, and 3D design which is suitable from manufacturing and retail to healthcare and education.

Various Devices of Mixed Reality

Microsoft come up with a reality called Windows Mixed Reality. This special version of Windows 10 is designed to combine VR and AR experiences with the Windows 10 operating system. the device that is used for Mixed Reality, is HoloLens.
